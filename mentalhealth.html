What is Mental Health?

Mental health is vital in ensuring the wellness of people. Mental health has been defined as the state of mental wellbeing that affect an individual's day to day activity including managing stress, realizing potentials, and positively impacting society. An individual with stable mental health is viewed as a healthy person who can positively impact people, community, and the society at large. Mental health is a priority and a basic human right as highlighted by the World Health Organization, and an integral part of human beings in their decision making processes. Different people experience mental health challenges in regard to how they cope with stresses like trauma, anxiety, and panic attacks. Mental health determines different social and clinical outcomes of people depending on their degree of mental stability. Mental health and mental stability of people is based on different variables that affect them.

Determinants of Mental Health

Different social, environmental, psychological, and structural determinants can individually or collectively combine to determine people's mental health and stability. These fators might include but are not limited to;

  • Psychological and Biological Factors
  • Social and Cultural Factors
  • Economical Factors
  • Geopolitical Factors
  • Environmental Factors

The determinants stated above can negatively trigger the mental health and stability of a person depending on how they perceive the triggers hence making them develop different mental health disorders as seen further elaborated here.
