Types of Mental Disorders

There are different types of mental disorders where these are considered to be the major disorders that encompass others in terms of how they relate to each other.

  1. Eating Disorders
  2. Anxiety Related Disorders
  3. Depressive Disorders
  4. Personality Disorders
  5. Psychotic Disorders

Eating Disorders

People with eating disorders are seen to have a serious mental condition in how they express their eating behaviours depending on what they are feeling or thinking. These people focus on what they eat, the amount of food they eat, their body weight, size, and shape. Examples of these disorders include;

  • Aneroxia
  • Obesity
  • Bulimia
  • Binge-eating disorder
  • Other Specified Feeding and Eating Disorders (OSFED)

Anxiety Related Disorders

Anxiety related disorders are mental disorders whose main characteristics are emotive including fear, anxiety, and panic that negatively affect the daily lives of people suffering from these disorders. These disorders include;

  • Panic Disorder
  • Phobias
  • Separation Anxiety Disorder
  • Social Anxiety Disorder
  • Obsessive Compulsive Disorder

Depressive Disorders

Depressive disorders are conditions that affect a person's mood with intense emotions of either hyperactivity or persistent feelings of sadness. Examples include;

  • Persistent Depressive Disorder
  • Clinical Depression
  • Postpartum Depression
  • Bipolar Disorder

Personality Disorders

Personality disorders are mental conditions that change how a person feels by differing from their average selves in terms of how they behave, feel, and relate with other people. These include;

  • Borderline Personality Disorder
  • Histrionic Personality Disorder
  • Narcissistic Personality Disorder
  • Antisocial Personality Disorder

Psychotic Disorders

Psychotic disorders make people suffering from these disorders to disassociate themselves with reality. Examples of psychotic disorders include;
  • Schizophrenia
  • Schizoaffective Disorder
